I work in logic and the philosophy of mathematics. I am professor of philosophy at the University of Lorraine and director of the Archives Poincaré (CNRS UMR 7117), a research laboratory in the history and philosophy of science.

Here are some articles I've written.

  • “On the depth of Szemerédi's theorem”. Philosophia Mathematica 23:2 (2015), pp. 163-176. This article is part of a special issue on mathematical depth, all of whose articles plus forward and afterward are free to access here.
  • “Plane and solid geometry: a note on purity of methods” (with Paolo Mancosu). In From Logic to Practice : Italian Studies in the Philosophy of Mathematics, edited by Gabriele Lolli, Marco Panza, and Giorgio Venturi, Springer (2015), pp. 23-31.
  • “Purity in arithmetic: some formal and informal issues.” In Formalism and Beyond. On the Nature of Mathematical Discourse, edited by Godehard Link, De Gruyter (2014), pp. 315-336.
  • “On the relationship between plane and solid geometry” (with Paolo Mancosu). Review of Symbolic Logic 5:2 (2012), 294-353. Preprint here.
  • “Geometria piana e solida: una nota sulla purezza del metodo” (with Paolo Mancosu). Notae Philosophicae Scientiae Formalis 1:1 (2012), 89-102.
  • “L'infinité des numbers premiers : une étude de cas de la pureté des méthodes”. Les études philosophiques 2:97 (2011).
